Who Is Robby Hoffman?

Hoffman was born in Brooklyn, New York, and later raised in Montreal, Canada, by a single mother. She was the seventh of ten siblings. Hoffman’s upbringing was deeply rooted in Hasidic traditions. Her academic journey led her to McGill University, where she studied accounting and communications. However, a few hours into her first day of the graduate CPA program at McGill, she quit and pursued a comedy career.

Robby Hoffman Career Highlights

Hoffman’s career took off when she began writing and performing stand-up comedy. 2018 she was a headlining act for the New York Comedy Festival. The following year, she recorded her first one-hour comedy special, “I’m Nervous,” at the Just For Laughs comedy festival in Toronto for Crave TV.

Hoffman’s first television writing job was for the PBS series Odd Squad, which won her a 2019 Daytime Emmy Award. She was also a Workin’ Moms and Baroness Von Sketch Show staff writer. After the cancellation of The Chris Gethard Show, where she was a staff writer, Hoffman starred in episodes of The Chris Gethard Show with Robby Hoffman on both MNN and Chris Gethard Presents. She also took the show live to the Montreal Just For Laughs festival.

In 2021, it was announced that “Rivkah,” an autobiographical comedy series created by and starring Hoffman, was developing with Showtime and A24.

Robby Hoffman Personal Life

Hoffman is Jewish and identifies as queer. According to her, her family was supportive when she came out as gay, and she maintains a close relationship with them. In August 2023, Hoffman announced that she is dating The Bachelorette alum Gabby Windey.
