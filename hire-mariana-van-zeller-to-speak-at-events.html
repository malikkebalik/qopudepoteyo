Mariana van Zeller, born in Cascais, Portugal on May 7, 1976, is a well-known Portuguese journalist and correspondent best known for her work with the National Geographic Channel. She has established herself as a respected personality in the area of journalism with an amazing career spanning several media channels.

Van Zeller studied international relations at the Universidade Lusada de Lisboa. She obtained important experience working at SIC, Portugal's main private television network, after graduating. She began her career at the network's main channel's travel and international departments before moving on to SIC Notcias, the network's news channel.

Her ambitions prompted her to apply to the Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism for a master's degree. Her determination shone through despite initial rejections. Van Zeller's perseverance paid off when she addressed the dean and persuaded him of her interest in journalism. The dean, impressed by her determination, granted her enrollment, paving the way for her voyage to the United States.

Van Zeller found herself at the center of a pivotal event in history, the September 11 attacks, shortly after arriving in New York City. She was phoned by a producer from SIC Notcias and put into a live reporting post atop the CBS Building in Midtown Manhattan. She rose to the occasion, well conscious of the gravity of the situation, and delivered the news to a nation watching her with bated breath.

Van Zeller moved to London after finishing her studies at Columbia, where she engaged herself in documentary production. She traveled to Syria to study Arabic at Damascus University in order to cover the Iraq War, boosting her abilities to unearth stories in the Middle East. Her compelling freelance documentaries from Syria were aired on prestigious platforms such as PBS's Frontline/World, the CBC, and Channel 4 in the United Kingdom..
