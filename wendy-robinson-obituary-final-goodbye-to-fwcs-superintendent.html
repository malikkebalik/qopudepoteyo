Wendy Robinson Obituary: Final Goodbye to FWCS Superintendent

 Posted on August 31, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  393 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Dr. Wendy Robinson, Fort Wayne Community Schools superintendent has died. Robinson, a supporter of public education worked as an educator in the district for 47 years. Before retiring in 2020, she held the role of FWCS superintendent for 17 years after being appointed to it in 2003.

Wendy Robinson Obituary: An Enduring Tribute to Beautiful Soul

Mayor Tom Henry said, “I’m saddened to learn of the sudden passing of Dr. Wendy Robinson.”

Kathy posted, “Wendy Robinson was named Indiana Superintendent of the Year in 2017. We were all so proud of her! Imagine our surprise when we arrived at the venue in Indianapolis and were wearing the same dress!”

Howard Stevenson, “I’m sad to hear of the passing of Dr. Wendy Robinson. She spent a total of 47 years as an educator with Ft.Wayne Community Schools.”

T.R. Snider posted, “So very sorry to hear about Wendy Robinson passing away. I coached Jason and my heart goes out to him and the entire family.”

Robinson was a pioneer since she was the first graduate woman to hold the position of district superintendent in FWCS. Robinson attended DePauw University after completing his high school education at Central. Robinson went on to receive a doctorate from Ball State University, two master’s degrees from Purdue Fort Wayne (then known as IPFW) and other degrees.

Robinson was selected Indiana’s Superintendent of the Year among many other honours in 2018. Robinson said in an interview:

“I don’t see this as something for me. I see it as a recognition of the work that happens in this district every single day. And that makes it more important that people recognize there are great things happening in the district because of the people in it.”

Involved in the neighborhood, Robinson served on many boards including Parkview Health and Learning Forward. The Family and Community Engagement Center for the school district, which Robinson assisted in establishing in 2016, was called in her honor in 2022.
